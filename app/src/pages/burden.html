The Rest of the World

The regulations that allow for the settlement of refugees in the United States have tightened dramatically since the Sept. 11, 2001 attacks, especially for those seeking resettlement from volatile Middle East countries. The current political climate and the 2008 financial crisis have not helped matters, as debates over immigration continue to rage in Washington. As a result, out of more than 4 million Syrian refugees, the United States has taken in fewer than 1,000.
